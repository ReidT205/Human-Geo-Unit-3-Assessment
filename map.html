Key locations, flows, and patterns in fashion appropriation

Map of Cultural Appropriation in Fashion

This map illustrates key location related to cultural appropriation in the fashion industry. It highlights the origins of cultural elements that are frequently appropriated and the major fashion industry centers where appropriation occurs.

Key Locations

Navajo Nation, USA

The Navajo Nation is the origin of Indigenous patterns, such as woven designs, that are frequently appropriated by fashion brands without permission or credit. These patterns hold deep cultural and spiritual significance for the Navajo people, symbolizing their history and identity.

Paris, France

Paris, a global fashion capital, is a city where designers showcase appropriated cultural elements on runways during Paris Fashion Week, influencing worldwide trends. High-profile events here have sparked controversy for turning sacred symbols into fashion statements.

H&M Stores Globally

H&M represents the fast-fashion retail system, with stores worldwide that mass-produce and sell appropriated designs. This global network spreads cultural symbols far from their origins, often without context or acknowledgment, turning them into commodified trends.

Instagram

Instagram is a platform where cultural appropriation is both displayed—through influencer outfits—and debated, via protest hashtags like #CulturalAppropriation. It shapes global cultural discourse, amplifying both the spread of appropriation and resistance to it.

New York Fashion Week, USA

New York Fashion Week is a major event where designers present collections, sometimes featuring appropriated cultural symbols detached from their roots. These shows set global trends, amplifying the reach of appropriation and raising questions about cultural ownership.

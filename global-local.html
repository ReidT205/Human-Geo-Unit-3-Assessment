Fashion appropriation often starts with local cultural symbols, like traditional Navajo patterns, being adopted by designers and commodified for global markets. This process can disconnect the symbol from its cultural roots, turning it into a mass-produced trend sold worldwide, which raises questions about ownership and whether globalization erases or reinvents cultural meanings.
